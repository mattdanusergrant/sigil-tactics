SIGIL TACTICS

v0.21 — 5v5 · 11-hero roster · 88-card pool (4 STD / 3 EPIC / 1 LEG per hero) · per-side Initiative deck

Modes

  • Campaign: start with Knight, Ranger, Mage. Defeat each of 6 boss heroes (Paladin, Druid, Scout, Berserker, Warlock, Assassin) to add them to your roster. Progress is saved between sessions.
  • Skirmish: single match using the full 9-hero roster, random AI draft.

Match flow

  • Draft (skirmish only): 1-2-2-2-2-1 snake — alternating snake draft, 10 picks total. Each side ends with 5 heroes from the 11-hero active roster.
  • Team select (campaign only): in the mission intro, tap to pick 5 heroes from your unlocked pool. AI's team is the boss plus themed allies from a mission-specific priority list. No draft phase.
  • Deploy: alternating face-down placement in reverse-of-draft order (P2 places first to offset P1's first-pick advantage). You see where the opponent placed but not which hero is in each tile — units flip face-up when play begins.
  • Play: standard initiative-order combat.

Rules

  • Win: defeat all enemy heroes (5v5 — both sides field 5 drafted heroes).
  • Initiative deck: each round, each side builds their own deck — every living unit contributes Init copies of itself (slow units: 1, normal: 2, fast: 3). Decks are shuffled. Sides alternate drawing one token at a time; whoever draws acts. Round ends when both decks are empty.
  • Facing: attacks from the SIDE deal ×1.5, REAR ×2 (Assassin: ×2 / ×2.5).
  • Terrain: ▲ high ground gives +1 ATK while standing on it.
  • Cards: start with 5 in hand, no max. Draw 1 at the end of every turn (yours or the AI's). Each card is one-shot.
  • Cycle: once per turn, a unit may forfeit its move and discard 2 cards to draw 1 — useful for digging past dead-source cards.
  • Cast by source hero: every card belongs to one of your 5 drafted heroes. It can only be played on that hero's turn — and only if that hero is still alive. Killing an enemy hero locks out their 8 cards (the entire suite they brought to the deck).
  • Range: damage cards target enemies within the casting hero's range (the same range they use for normal attacks). Ally-target cards (heal/shield/charge/buff) reach any of your allies.
  • Opportunity cost: playing a card counts as the unit's action for the turn. You may still move first either way; you cannot play a card after attacking or healing.
  • Shield (⛨N): absorbs damage before HP. Persists across rounds.
  • ATK buff (+NA): bonus damage that clears at round start.
  • Move buff: extra movement on the buffed unit's next turn only.
  • HP dice: every unit's max HP is a polyhedral die face count (4/6/8/10/12/20).

Card categories

  • Equipment (5 per hero) — one each of Weapon, Armor, Helmet, Boots, Artifact.
  • Actions (5 per hero) — moves, attacks, and effects performed by a hero. Spells are a subtype of action (purple stripe, tag SPL) — mechanically identical to actions, distinguished only by flavor.
  • Your deck: 30 cards built at deploy time from your 3 drafted heroes' suites. Pool across the 9 active heroes is 90 cards total.

Card kinds (effects)

No pure buffs/debuffs — every card causes something this turn. Some compound kinds also carry a lasting secondary effect.

  • dmg — N damage to target enemy (shield absorbs first)
  • dmg-adj — N damage to target + S splash to orthogonal adjacent enemies
  • dmg-vuln — N damage + target gains vulnerable (next attack on them does +V extra)
  • dmg-self-buff — N damage + caster +B ATK this round
  • dmg-self-charge — N damage + caster +M extra move this turn
  • dmg-self-heal — N damage + caster heals H HP (lifesteal)
  • dmg-team-buff — N damage + every ally +B ATK this round
  • heal — restore N HP to target ally
  • heal-all — restore N HP to every ally
  • heal-shield — heal target ally + add S shield to them
  • heal-buff — heal target ally + ally +B ATK this round
  • heal-all-buff — heal all allies + all allies +B ATK this round
  • shield — +N shield on target ally
  • shield-all — +N shield on every ally
  • charge — target ally +N move this turn

Vulnerable is a counter on the target enemy. The next attack against them adds the vuln value to its damage, then clears the counter. Vuln also clears at round start.
